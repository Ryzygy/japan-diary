DAY 01 Arrival Day
Narita → Asakusa → Ginza
Landed at Narita, dropped bags, and immediately headed out. First stop was Senso-ji, the gates, the whole thing. Then Ginza to feel the contrast. Classic first day in Tokyo.
DAY 02 Shinjuku Day
Shinjuku & Suga Shrine
Spent the day in Shinjuku — the chaos, the crowds, the streets. Made a detour to Suga Shrine, famously featured in Your Name. Smaller than expected but worth every step.
DAY 03 Akihabara Day
Akihabara
Electric Town. Arcades, figures, anime shops, electronics everywhere. Could have spent three days here. The kind of place that's overwhelming in the best possible way.
DAY 04 Shibuya Day
Shibuya
The crossing at rush hour. The scramble is something else when you're in the middle of it — hundreds of people crossing from every direction at once, somehow without collision.
DAY 05 Back to Shibuya
Shibuya & Ikebukuro
More Shibuya in the morning, then up to Ikebukuro in the afternoon. Different energy — less tourist, more local. Found some good spots tucked away from the main streets.
DAY 06 Day Trip
Echigo Yuzawa
Took the Shinkansen out to Echigo Yuzawa — a complete change of pace from Tokyo. Snow country, onsen town, and the setting of Kawabata's novel. Quiet in a way Tokyo never is.
DAY 07 Last Full Day
Asakusa
Last full day, walked the same streets from day one but noticed different things. This was also the day I finally bought my pasalubong, taking my time choosing what to bring home. The kind of place you could keep returning to and still find something new.
DAY 08 Asakusa, Akihabara, Narita
Last Day in Tokyo
Since checkout was still at 2PM and Tonkatsu Oribe opens at 11AM, I spent the morning in Akihabara playing arcade games one last time. After my final meal at Tonkatsu Oribe, I went back to Asakusa, grabbed my luggage, and made my way to Narita. Quiet train ride, full heart, already thinking about coming back.
